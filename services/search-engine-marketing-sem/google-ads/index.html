// 01 — What this is

Google Ads is infrastructure.
Treat it like one.

Most businesses treat Google Ads like a faucet — turn it on, hope leads come out. The accounts that compound over time are built differently: clean structure, airtight tracking, and strategy tied to actual business economics.

Search Engine Marketing (SEM)

SEM is paid placement inside Google's results — you bid to appear when someone is actively searching for what you offer. Done right, it's the highest-intent traffic channel available. Done wrong, it's just expensive noise.

Google Ads — the platform

A pay-per-click system spanning Search, Shopping, Display, YouTube, Performance Max, and Demand Gen. Each campaign type has a different job. The operator's job is knowing which one to deploy, when, at what spend level — then letting the data drive what happens next.

No black boxes.
Here's the exact process.

Before anything is built, I tear down what's already running. Wasted spend, broken tracking, misaligned bid strategies, account structure debt — all of it surfaces in the first audit. You get a clear picture of where the money is going and why results are lagging.

Tracking and measurement foundation

If the data going into Google Ads is wrong, everything downstream is wrong. I build or rebuild the tracking layer first — GA4, GTM, server-side tagging, enhanced conversions, and CRM integration — before optimizing a single bid.

Campaign architecture and launch

Keyword research, match type strategy, ad group structure, RSA copy, bidding framework, budget allocation — built around your unit economics, not a generic template. Then we launch with tight controls and a defined ramp period.

Systematic optimization

Weekly search term analysis, negative keyword pruning, bid adjustments, Quality Score improvement, A/B testing on ad copy, and landing page recommendations. This is ongoing work — not a set-it-and-hope-it-holds approach.

Reporting you'll actually read

Looker Studio dashboards built for founders, not media buyers. You see the metrics that connect to business outcomes — leads, revenue, cost per acquisition — without the vanity impressions fluff that agencies use to fill slides.

// 04 — Campaign types

Every format serves
a different job.

Throwing everything into one campaign type is how you waste budget. Here's what each format is actually built for.

→ search

Search Ads

High-intent queries. Best for lead gen and direct response. Your floor.

→ pmax

Performance Max

AI-driven full-funnel. Powerful when signals are clean. Dangerous when they're not.

→ shopping

Shopping Ads

E-commerce native. Feed quality and bid strategy determine everything.

→ display

Display Ads

Brand awareness and remarketing. Volume channel, not a conversion driver.

→ video

YouTube / Video

Demand creation at scale. Skippable, non-skippable, and video action formats.

→ retargeting

Remarketing

Bring back warm visitors. Highest-efficiency spend when audiences are built right.
