The Business Owner Lens

I run a business myself. That changes everything about how I look at data.

Most analysts report metrics. I report one thing that actually matters to an owner:

The Only Question That Matters
After every expense — fulfillment, staff, tools, overhead — and after the ad spend… are we actually making money?
ROAS Revenue ÷ Ad Spend
What platforms report. Looks great. Means nothing alone.
A 4x ROAS on a product with 20% margins after COGS, shipping, and refunds is a money-losing campaign. Platforms don't tell you that.
⚠ Starting point only
CAC Total Ad Spend ÷ New Customers
How much you actually paid to acquire one customer — including all wasted spend.
Compare this against LTV (lifetime value). If CAC > LTV, the business bleeds with every new customer. If CAC < LTV, you have a machine.
◈ Core health metric
ROI (Revenue − Total Costs) ÷ Total Costs
The full picture: revenue minus everything — product cost, fulfillment, staff, tools, AND ad spend.
This is the number I care about. Not gross revenue. Not attributed conversions. Net profit after every dollar out the door.
✓ The real number
Incremental ROI What ads actually caused vs. would have happened anyway
Isolating the true lift from paid ads — removing organic sales, brand searches, and returning customers that would have converted regardless.
Most brands are over-crediting their ads. Incrementality tests reveal if cutting budget loses revenue or just cleans up the reporting.
◈ Incrementality testing
MER Total Revenue ÷ Total Ad Spend (all channels)
Marketing Efficiency Ratio — the blended view across every paid channel at once.
Used with Triple Whale. When platform ROAS doesn't match business reality, MER is the north star that cuts through attribution noise and tells you if marketing is working as a whole.
✓ North star for scaling
Bottom Line Do we profit?
Revenue − COGS − Fulfillment − Staff − Tools − Overhead − Ad Spend = Actual Profit
I build reporting that shows this number clearly — not buried in a dashboard, not hidden behind a 6x ROAS headline. The owner should be able to open a report and see: "We made $X this month after everything."
✓ How I report to owners
