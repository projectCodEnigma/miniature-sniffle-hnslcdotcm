Neither Facebook Ads nor Google Ads is universally better

Each platform serves its own distinct audience and purpose

They do not directly compete with each other

In my experience running campaigns on both platforms for different businesses, here is what actually works:

G

Google Ads

Operates on its strong search network. It captures high intent users who are actively looking for solutions. People typing exact queries like plumber near me or buy running shoes online.

Key Strength

Higher conversion rates and better quality leads or sales

f

Facebook Ads

Reaches its own unique audience of people browsing feeds, stories and reels. It excels at building awareness, creating interest through visuals and storytelling.

Key Strength

Cheaper traffic and more volume

Important Distinction

Facebook content and ads generally do not get indexed by Google in a way that competes with Google Ads. The two platforms have clear distinctions — one is based on active search intent, while the other is based on audience behavior and creative engagement.

Google Ads

  • ✓ Higher intent and stronger conversions
  • ✕ Clicks usually cost more

Facebook Ads

  • ✓ Lower cost per click and broader reach
  • ✕ Often requires more nurturing to convert

My Recommendation

The most effective strategy is using both together in a full funnel approach. Facebook builds awareness and warms up audiences, while Google captures ready to buy searchers. This combination delivers better overall results than relying on just one platform.
