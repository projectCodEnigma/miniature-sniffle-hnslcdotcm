Operating Principles

How I Operate

I build acquisition infrastructure for founders scaling eCommerce, healthcare/medspa, B2B SaaS, and high-intent lead gen. Not as a vendor. Not as a campaign manager. You get predictable, compounding ROAS — because I fix tracking, audit Performance Max, and build first-party data systems before scaling spend.

10+ years. $550M+ attributed revenue. 50%+ average CPA reduction across verticals.

What I Own

Tracking & Measurement (The Foundation)

Your campaigns are only as good as your data. I set up conversion tracking that actually works: server-side implementation, offline import, enhanced conversions, and GA4 validation. This isn't optional — it's the floor. Without it, you're flying blind.

Performance Max Architecture

PMax is Google's future, but "set and forget" kills budgets. I structure focused asset groups with strong audience signals, build downside protection into every campaign, and audit for redundancy that bleeds money. The goal: predictable baseline ROAS with explosive upside once systems lock in.

First-Party Data as Your Moat

Platform deprecations, algorithm changes, iOS updates — they're coming. I build your shield: Customer Match audiences, consent-based customer data, CLV/profit signals, and offline CRM integration. This data stays yours and compounds over time.

Budget Discipline & Scaling Logic

I don't burn your money testing random things. Every dollar has a reason. I scale what works, pause what doesn't, protect downside before chasing upside, and give you clear signals for when to double down. Your ROAS trend matters more than your peak ROAS.

Founder-Focused Reporting

You get: actual spend, tracked revenue/pipeline, true ROAS/CPA, data quality health score, and month-over-month trajectory. No inflated dashboards. No vanity metrics. Just what matters — so you know exactly where budget is flowing and whether systems are compounding.

What I Do Not Do

  • Push buttons without fixing structure. I don't run campaigns on broken tracking or weak signals. Foundation first, scaling second.
  • "Set and forget" Performance Max. These campaigns leak budget through redundancy, weak audiences, and misaligned asset groups. If it's on, it's monitored and audited.
  • Chase vanity metrics. Pretty dashboards, impressions, reach, engagement — these are distractions. Real metrics: ROAS, CPA, pipeline velocity, data quality.
  • Weekly reassurance calls. I report clearly and early. If something is broken, you hear about it immediately. If results are tracking, you see the trend.
  • Work with teams avoiding hard conversations. If your tracking is incomplete, your data is messy, or your first-party audience strategy is weak — we address it head-on. No lip service.

How Decisions Are Made

Every decision starts with data. If the data is bad, we fix it first. If it's clean, we use it to architect systems that compound.

The sequence:

  1. Audit and fix conversion tracking and first-party data readiness
  2. Architect Performance Max with focused asset groups and strong audience signals
  3. Build downside protection into budget allocation
  4. Scale upside once systems are proven stable
  5. Report clearly on whether the infrastructure is compounding

If something is broken — PMax redundancy, incomplete offline conversions, weak consent modeling, GA4 misconfiguration — it gets fixed directly and early. No technical debt. No workarounds.
