Common questions 06 items

Why is there a $5,000/month minimum ad spend?

Below $5K, data volume is too thin to optimize meaningfully. This threshold ensures there's enough signal to make decisions that actually compound over time — and that the engagement is worthwhile for both sides.

Do you track hours?

No. The fee is outcome-based, not hour-based. What matters is campaign performance, not how much time was logged in a tracker.

Can we structure this as a flat retainer or a hybrid?

Yes — and this comes up often. A flat monthly retainer works well for brands that need cost predictability and a defined scope. A retainer + % of ad spend hybrid is available when both floor stability and scale alignment matter. No client situation is identical, and the fee structure should reflect that. Raise it on the fit call.

How fast is onboarding?

Most accounts are live within 7 days of the fit call. Conversion tracking, campaign structure, and reporting setup happen in the first week — not strung across a month of kickoffs.

Who do you work with?

U.S.-based ecommerce brands, DTC operators, and B2B lead generation businesses. The common thread: proven product-market fit and a willingness to treat paid acquisition as infrastructure, not an experiment.

Is there a long-term contract?

Engagements are structured for 90-day minimum cycles — enough runway to build systems that produce compounding results. After that, month-to-month. No year-long lock-ins.
